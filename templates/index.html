Overview

I decided to log some of my trash to reflect upon my possible wasting of resources; a corner people often unknowingly place themselves in where they discard items, oblivious to their possible use after being recycled.

Convenience

Some of my items fall into a group of nonnecessities that are only primarily used to enlighten my livelihood. These include salt spray to better control my long hair, earbuds to listen to music or audio in noisy areas and better focus, plastic utensils so I can avoid eating with my fingers, and over-the-counter painkillers for that occasional headache. I restock my salt spray monthly and am a fan of the volume it adds to my hair. I almost never replace my earbuds - the ones pictured are a secondary pair where the bud I have doesn't work and the other I lost - and was surprised to notice my ibuprofen was expired.

Necessities

Other items are human necessities whose lifespan and use ran out and should be replaced. These are the fairly useless apple core and banana peel and face mask, the latter being safe to be considered a necessity for some locations in this current time period. I enjoy these fruits daily and wear masks where required, i.e. in healthcare settings. I have many memorable moments devouring bananas after my cross country races, they're actually some pretty good motivation to finish faster!

Wellness

My final two items; a Nike sock with holes beyond repair (can't be donated), and receipt from prescription toothpaste fall into the common category of improving my health and wellbeing. The former obviously aids in day-to-day walking and traveling alongside exercise and running. The toothpaste (extra fluoride) tries to deal with my poor dental genetics to keep my teeth in pristine condition.
